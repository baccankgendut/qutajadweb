Career

In 1976, Jonathan released his debut record on Bearsville Records as “the Jonathan Cain Band, Windy City Breakdown.”

In 1979, he became a member of “The Babys” and performed on their two significant albums as “On the Edge,” and “Union Jacks.”

In 1980, Jonathan left the group “The Babys” and joined the rock band named “Journey.” He took the place of Gregg Rolie on keyboards.

A few years later, Jonathan again reunited with his former bandmates Ricky Phillips, Neal Schon, and John Waite of the group “The Babys.” With the support of his fellow band members, he formed the band “Bad English.” This band launched two albums but got terminated in 1990.

In 1996, he reunited with the line-up of the rock band “Journey” They reformed as well as recorded their album “Trial by Fire.”

Jonathan is seen to performs piano as a solo artist at every Journey event. He began this tradition at the time of joining the rock band in 1980.
