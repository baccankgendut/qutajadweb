Creed Bratton Grass Roots

Bratton played lead guitar, Rick Coonce played drums, Entner played rhythm guitar, and Kenny Fukomoto played bass on the 13th Floor group. Later, it lost its bass player to the draft and quickly recruited Rob Grill, changing its name to The Grass Roots for prior name recognition.

In 1967, the group went straight to the top 10 with the song “Let’s Live for Today”. The group proceeded in its hit-making vein and toured the United States. Iconic hit songs like “Midnight Confessions” have cemented the band ‘s status as significant contributors to the rock music scene.

The Grass Roots had top songwriters giving them their best songs, and they themselves composed other songs. Music on the recordings was performed on its big songs by musicians from LA studio, known as The Wrecking Crew. Bratton co-wrote the songs “Beatin’ Round the Bush”, “No Exit”, and “Hot Bright Lights”, and self-composed “Dinner for Eight” and “House of Stone”.

On “This Precious Time” and “Dinner for Eight” he sang lead vocals. On his albums Let’s Live for Today, Feelings, Golden Grass (a compilation), and Lovin’ Things, Bratton performed with the group. Three of the charted albums, and Golden Grass was certified as a gold record. He participated in ten of the group’s singles, of which eight were charted; “Midnight Confessions” received a gold record certificate.
