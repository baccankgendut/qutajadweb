The Story Behind “I Am Redeemed”

Jessy Dixon, born in San Antonio, Texas in 1938, was a gospel singer, songwriter, and pianist. His career began in the late 1960s when he toured with several notable gospel bands such as the James Cleveland Singers and the Chicago Community Choir.

In 1979, Dixon released his album “The Wicked Shall Cease from Troubling,” which included “I Am Redeemed.” The song became an instant hit in the gospel world and has since become a staple in many churches around the world.

Dixon wrote the song during a time in his life when he was struggling with addiction to drugs and alcohol. He said in an interview that he was “feeling very low and down” when he prayed and asked God for deliverance.

The lyrics of “I Am Redeemed” reflect Dixon’s redemption and deliverance from his addictions and struggles. The song speaks to the power of the blood of Jesus Christ and its ability to cleanse and save a person from sin. It’s a message of hope and joy that has resonated with countless people over the years.

The Lyrics of “I Am Redeemed”

The lyrics of “I Am Redeemed” are simple yet powerful. The chorus goes as follows:

The words “I am redeemed” are repeated throughout the song, emphasizing the message of redemption and highlighting the fact that it is through Jesus Christ that we are saved.
